HR Policies & Employee Handbook FAQs

Practical answers to how we structure, draft and implement your people governance documents.

What types of HR policies can you help us develop?

We support full HR policy suites, including recruitment and selection, probation and confirmation, working hours and leave, compensation and benefits, performance management, code of conduct, disciplinary and grievance procedures, equal opportunity, hybrid/remote work, data protection, health and safety and more.

What is the difference between an HR policy manual and an employee handbook?

The HR policy manual is a detailed internal reference for HR and management, while the employee handbook is an accessible, employee-facing guide that focuses on what staff need to know, remember and act on. We build both to work together.

How long does a typical project take?

A focused update to existing policies may take 3–6 weeks. A full-scope project—framework design, policy drafting, handbook development and rollout planning—may take 6–10 weeks depending on complexity and the speed of stakeholder feedback.

Can you align policies with our local labour laws and sector regulations?

Yes. We design policies to reflect relevant labour and employment regulations and sector guidance. We also encourage collaboration with your legal advisers for jurisdiction-specific review, which we can coordinate as part of the project.

How do you keep documents from being too legalistic?

We balance legal soundness with clarity. We use plain language, examples, process maps and visual cues where appropriate, so that managers and employees can understand and apply the content without getting lost in technical terms.

How often should HR policies and handbooks be reviewed?

We recommend light updates at least once a year and full reviews every 2–3 years—or sooner if there are material changes in law, business model, technology, working patterns or structure. We can help you define a review calendar and governance checklist.

Data Protection

HR policies and employee handbooks often reference sensitive topics, including performance, discipline, conduct and personal information. We treat all underlying data and documents with strict confidentiality.

Only authorised CICL team members working on your engagement have access to your drafts, internal policies and related HR data. Information is transmitted over secure channels where applicable and stored in systems with least-privilege access.

We collect and process only the information required to design and refine your policies and handbook, and we retain working materials for the minimum period needed to support implementation and agreed review cycles.

Any examples or anonymised extracts used for training, benchmarking or thought leadership purposes are carefully redacted to remove names, company identifiers and sensitive details.

Our goal is to give you robust people governance documents without compromising the privacy and dignity of the individuals whose data informs them.
