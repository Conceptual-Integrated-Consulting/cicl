Business Model & Structure Design FAQs

How we scope, design and support structural change in your organisation.

When is the right time to revisit our business model?

Triggers often include rapid growth, margin pressure, new regulations, acquisitions, entering new markets, launching new product lines or persistent execution challenges under the current structure.

How involved do our leaders need to be?

Effective design cannot be fully outsourced. We typically work closely with sponsors and a cross-functional core team, with periodic working sessions and decision meetings with senior leadership.

Will this lead to layoffs or major restructuring?

Not necessarily. Some projects focus on clarifying roles, decision rights and governance within existing headcount. Where structural changes are recommended, we help you plan them thoughtfully and transparently.

Do you work with HR on role design and change management?

Yes. We often partner with HR to refine roles, support internal communication and integrate the new design into performance management, job descriptions and talent plans.

Can you coordinate with our external advisors?

We can collaborate with your legal, tax, audit or transaction advisors to ensure the model and structure also align with regulatory, compliance and transaction realities.

How do we keep the design alive after the project ends?

We help you establish review rhythms, dashboards and governance practices so the design is revisited periodically and refined as your context changes—not treated as a one-off exercise.

Data Protection & Confidentiality

Business model and structure work often touches sensitive information: financials, strategy documents, internal performance data, pay bands, incentive structures and governance records. We handle all such information with strict confidentiality.

Access is limited to the CICL project team working directly on your engagement. Working files are stored in secure environments, and information is shared with your stakeholders through agreed, secure channels.

We minimise the personal data we collect and avoid including individual identifiers in design artefacts unless explicitly required. Where external advisors or partners are involved, they operate under appropriate confidentiality and data-handling agreements.

Any anonymised examples used in future training or marketing materials are carefully redacted so they cannot be traced back to your organisation.

Our goal is simple: you can speak candidly about your current model and structure, knowing that your information is protected throughout the engagement.
