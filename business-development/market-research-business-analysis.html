Market Research & Business Analysis FAQs

How we scope, execute and protect your research and analysis projects.

What types of research projects can CICL support?

We support market sizing, opportunity assessment, customer and stakeholder insight, competitive and landscape analysis, pricing/packaging studies, feasibility assessments and pre-launch testing for new countries, products or business lines.

What research methods do you use?

We combine secondary (desk) research with primary data where needed: interviews, surveys, focus groups, stakeholder workshops and analysis of your internal data. The mix depends on your questions, timelines and budget.

How long does a typical engagement take?

Smaller, desk-based projects typically take 3–4 weeks. Larger engagements involving multiple countries, segments or extensive fieldwork may take 6–10 weeks or longer, with milestones agreed upfront.

Can you work with our internal data and analytics team?

Yes. We often combine market intelligence with your CRM, financial or operational data. We can plug into your analytics, product or finance teams to validate assumptions and ensure the final outputs fit into your existing reporting.

Do you only cover specific industries or regions?

We work across multiple sectors and geographies. For highly specialised or technical markets, we may bring in subject-matter experts or local partners to deepen the analysis.

How do you ensure that our information stays confidential?

All client data and internal documents are handled under strict confidentiality. We limit access to the core project team, use secure channels for sharing information and can sign NDAs or equivalent agreements with you and any external collaborators.

Data Protection & Confidentiality

Market research and business analysis often rely on sensitive internal information—financials, customer data, product roadmaps and strategic plans. We treat all such information with strict confidentiality.

Only authorised CICL team members assigned to your project have access to your data. We store working files in secure environments, apply least-privilege access principles and use encrypted channels where appropriate for file transfer and collaboration.

We minimise the personal data we collect, anonymise where possible and avoid including unnecessary identifiers in research outputs. Any third-party partners involved in fieldwork or specialised analysis work under clear confidentiality and data-handling agreements.

Examples, benchmarks or insights reused in other contexts are carefully anonymised to avoid revealing proprietary details about your business or your customers.

Our aim is simple: you get the depth of insight you need, without compromising the privacy, security or commercial sensitivity of your information.
