Corporate Branding FAQs

How we help you clarify, express and manage your corporate brand.

Do we need to change our name to work on our brand?

No. Most engagements focus on clarifying and strengthening the existing brand, not renaming. We only recommend name changes when they are clearly necessary for strategy, clarity or legal reasons.

Can you support both corporate and employer branding?

Yes. We often work across corporate brand, product brand and employer brand, making sure employees experience the same promise that customers hear.

How involved should our leadership team be?

Senior leaders play a key role in defining direction and making trade-offs. We design touchpoints so they can provide input and decisions without being overloaded by day-to-day execution.

Can you work alongside our creative or media agencies?

Yes. We frequently act as the strategic backbone, ensuring that campaigns and creative outputs from agencies align with the overarching brand platform and positioning.

What if we already have some brand materials?

We start by reviewing what you have—logos, taglines, decks, websites, campaigns—and then identify what can be refined, reused or retired rather than starting from zero.

How do we keep the brand alive after the project?

We focus on governance and enablement: guidelines, templates, training and ownership structures that make it easier to maintain consistency long after the engagement ends.

Data Protection & Brand Assets

Brand work often involves access to internal documents, strategic plans, design files and third-party assets. We treat these materials as confidential and use them only for the purposes agreed in the engagement.

Working files are shared through secure, approved channels and accessed only by CICL team members involved in your project. Where we collaborate with external creative partners, we operate under clear NDAs and asset usage agreements.

We respect intellectual property rights and clearly distinguish between your proprietary assets, third-party licensed content and CICL frameworks. Any generic examples used for training or marketing are anonymised and de-linked from your organisation.

Our goal is to protect your reputation and intellectual property while giving your teams the clarity and tools they need to apply the brand confidently.
