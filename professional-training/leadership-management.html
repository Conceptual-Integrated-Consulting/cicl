FAQs

Clear answers to common questions about CICL Leadership & Management programmes.

Who is this programme best suited for?

We design versions for emerging leaders, middle managers and senior leaders. Each track uses different cases and expectations, but all share a common leadership language and core principles.

Can we align the content with our existing values or frameworks?

Yes. We can integrate your leadership values, competency models or performance frameworks into the programme so it reinforces what you already use internally.

Do you provide certificates?

We can provide participation certificates where appropriate, but we emphasise behaviour change, feedback and on-the-job application over credentials alone.

How many participants can join a cohort?

Ideal cohort sizes usually range between 15 and 30 participants per group for high interaction. Larger audiences can be split into multiple cohorts or supported through a blended model.

Can you work with geographically distributed teams?

Yes. We support fully virtual, hybrid and multi-location cohorts using digital facilitation tools and structured pre- and post-session work.

What are the next steps if we’re interested?

We start with a short discovery call to clarify your context, leadership levels, timelines and budget. From there, we propose a tailored programme outline and delivery plan.

Data Protection

We treat all participant information, diagnostics and feedback with strict confidentiality. Only authorised CICL consultants and agreed internal stakeholders can access identifiable data.

Feedback forms, assessments and coaching notes are handled through secure channels and systems. Where possible, aggregated insights are shared with sponsors without exposing individual data.

Any case studies or anonymised examples used in future programmes are carefully stripped of names, client identifiers and sensitive details.

We retain participant data only for the period agreed at the start of the engagement, after which it is removed from our active systems in line with our data protection standards.

Our goal is to create a psychologically safe learning environment where leaders can reflect honestly and experiment without compromising their privacy.
