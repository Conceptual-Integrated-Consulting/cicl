Data challenges

Technology & data gaps we help you close

We focus on the real barriers that stop teams from using data—from spreadsheet chaos and siloed reports to unclear dashboards and low confidence with tools.

Spreadsheet overload

Important processes run on fragile spreadsheets with complex formulas, manual reconciliations and limited documentation.

Dashboards no one trusts

Multiple versions of the truth across reports and dashboards, limited adoption and stakeholders who struggle to interpret visuals.

Data scattered across systems

Data stored in several tools, exports and files with little standardisation, naming hygiene or clear ownership.

Low confidence with tools

Teams who underuse Excel, Power BI or analytics tools because they lack practical training tailored to their context and use-cases.

FAQs

Clear answers to common questions about CICL Technology & Data programmes.

Do participants need laptops and the relevant tools installed?

Yes. For most Technology & Data programmes, participants should have laptops with the required tools (e.g. Excel, Power BI, Python environment) installed before sessions begin.

Can you adapt to our existing BI or reporting tools?

In many cases, yes. We can adapt content to your stack as long as we understand the tools and have the right access to prepare examples and exercises.

How technical are your Technology & Data programmes?

We can run highly practical non-technical tracks for business users, as well as deeper technical tracks for analysts and data teams. We calibrate based on your audience and goals.

Can we start small with a pilot?

Yes. Many clients start with a pilot programme for one function or location, then extend to wider teams once the approach and impact have been validated.

Do you provide post-training support?

We can design follow-up clinics, office hours or coaching sessions to help teams embed new practices and refine dashboards or reports.

What are the next steps if we are interested?

We begin with a discovery conversation to understand your systems, teams and priorities. Based on that, we propose a programme outline, delivery format and investment options.

Data Protection

We recognise that Technology & Data training often involves reference to real or realistic data. Any sample data shared with CICL is treated as confidential and handled in line with your organisation’s data protection policies.

Where possible, we encourage anonymised or synthetic data that reflects your structures without exposing sensitive information. Access to any real data is restricted to authorised CICL consultants working on your engagement.

Training materials, lab files and example datasets are stored securely for the agreed duration of the engagement and follow-up. After this period, they are removed from our active systems unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Our goal is to create a safe environment where people can learn and practise with data, without compromising privacy, confidentiality or security.
